Cornbread Recipe


Description

Cornbread is a popular quick bread made from cornmeal. It can be savory or sweet, baked in a pan or skillet, and is often served as a versatile side dish.

Cornbread is a quick bread made with cornmeal, associated with the cuisine of the Southern United States, with origins in Native American cuisine. It is an example of batter bread. Dumplings and pancakes made with finely ground cornmeal are staple foods of the Hopi people in Arizona. The Hidatsa people of the Upper Midwest call baked cornbread naktsi, while the Choctaw people of the Southeast call it bvnaha. The Cherokee and Seneca tribes enrich the basic batter by adding chestnuts, sunflower seeds, apples, or berries, and sometimes combine it with beans or potatoes. Modern versions of cornbread are usually leavened by baking powder.

Directions

  1. Gather all the ingredients.
  2. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Grease a 9-inch round cake pan.
  3. Whisk together the flour, cornmeal, sugar, baking powder, and salt in a large bowl.
  4. Add the milk, vegetable oil, and egg; whisk until well combined.
  5. Pour the batter into the prepared pan.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • Cornmeal
  • ⅔ cup white sugar
  • Baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • Milk
  • Vegetable oil
  • 1 egg
